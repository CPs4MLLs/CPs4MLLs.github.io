Who We Are

Megan Madigan Peercy, Ph.D., is a Professor in the Department of Teaching and Learning, Policy and Leadership at the University of Maryland, where she teaches in two specializations: Applied Linguistics and Language Education, and Teacher Education and Professional Development. She also serves as Co-Director of the Multilingual Research Center. Her research examines pedagogies of teacher education and the preparation and career-long development of teachers who work with multilingual learners (MLLs). As a former MLL and Spanish teacher, she is deeply invested in bringing practice and theory into dialogue to support teachers and students. Her research has been funded by the Spencer Foundation, the Institute of Education Sciences, and the Maryland State Department of Education. Examples of her recent work appear in Linguistics and Education, Teaching and Teacher Education, TESOL Quarterly, and TESOL Journal. Her co-authored book Core Practices for Teaching Multilingual Students: Humanizing Pedagogies for Equity is an important resource for P-12 teachers of MLLs. Dr. Peercy is recipient of the American Educational Research Association Leadership Through Research Award, and Mid-Career Award, from the Second Language Research group. She has also been named a University of Maryland Distinguished Scholar-Teacher. She is co-author of Core Practices for Teaching Multilingual Students: Humanizing Pedagogies for Equity.
Johanna Tigert, Ph.D., is University Lecturer for the Department of Teacher Education at the University of Turku, Finland, where she works across multiple degree programs preparing classroom, early childhood, and craft education teachers. She identifies as a transnational scholar and teacher educator. She has MA degrees in Russian Language and Literature and in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL), as well as a doctorate in Curriculum and Instruction with a specialization in applied linguistics and language education. She has worked as a Finnish heritage language instructor, as an English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) teacher across multiple settings from K-12 schools to community colleges, and for the past decade, as a teacher educator in both the United States and Finland. Her research spans a wide variety of issues related to language education, including the development of the skills, knowledge, and dispositions of teachers in increasingly multilingual classrooms. Seeking to humanize the education of multilingual learners, her research focuses on ways language and culture can be brought to the forefront of classroom instruction, drawing on rich qualitative data gathered from policy documents, schools, teachers, and students. Her recent publications have appeared in Journal of Curriculum Studies and TESOL Journal. She is a coauthor of two books, Core practices for teaching multilingual students: Humanizing pedagogies for equity and Becoming language teacher educators: Critical friendships and communities.
Daisy Fredricks, Ph.D., is an Associate Professor of Teaching and Learning and Assistant Dean in the College of Education and Community Innovation at Grand Valley State University. She earned an MEd in Curriculum and Instruction with a focus in Bilingual and English as a Second Language (ESL) Education, and a PhD in Applied Linguistics from Arizona State University. Before entering higher education, Daisy served as a bilingual, ESL, and Structured English Immersion (SEI) teacher in elementary and middle school classrooms in Michigan, Texas, and Arizona. Her research explores teacher preparation, multilingual learner education, humanizing pedagogies, and core practices for teaching multilingual learners in PK–12 classrooms. She is dedicated to helping educators develop inclusive practices that respect and value the linguistic and cultural backgrounds of their students. Her recent work has appeared in TESOL Quarterly, Teaching and Teacher Education, TESOL Journal, and other peer-reviewed journals and books. She is co-author of Core Practices for Teaching Multilingual Students: Humanizing Pedagogies for Equity.

We are educators, researchers, and community partners focused on language and literacy, equity, and humanizing pedagogy. Our work was a collaboration between teacher educators, pre-service and in-service teachers, and graduate students.

Our Research

Our work centers the flourishing of multilingual students, families, and communities by focusing on the development of their teachers as humanizing practitioners. Using our core practices and supporting teachers’ humanizing understandings, we aim for all teachers to welcome students as whole human beings in their learning journeys. Key to this is teachers’ knowledge of how to move humanizing ideas and values into actionable practice, and we leverage core practices for multilingual students as a foundational way to support teachers in this.
